### **Parallel Dialer Scripts for High-Velocity Calling**

**Tone Directive:** Operator. Direct. Not salesy. Confident. John Kelly energy: "We're damn good at what we do."

---

### **SEGMENT 1: DOMAIN BUYERS**
*Target:* Corp Dev at SAP, Workday, Oracle, ADP, OpenAI, Anthropic, Deel, Rippling

**15-Second Opener:**
"Hi [Name], Ewing from Next Chapter. Quick question — is [Company] the right group for strategic domain acquisitions? I'm calling about **hr.com**."

**Value Prop (One Sentence):**
"We're running a controlled 60-day process for **hr.com**, the category-defining two-letter domain in the largest enterprise software vertical."

**One Qualifying Question:**
"Has [Company] ever looked at domain acquisitions as a core part of your platform or market strategy?"

**Meeting Set Close:**
"I'll send a one-page overview. Can we get 15 minutes with the right person on your team this week to walk through it?"

**Top 3 Objection Handles:**
1.  **"We don't buy domains."**
    *   "Understood. This isn't a domain play; it's a market control play. It's about owning the category entry point for every HR buyer searching online."
2.  **"Not in the budget / not a priority."**
    *   "That's why I'm calling now. The window is 60 days. A brief look now ensures you're not blindsided when a competitor secures it."
3.  **"Send me an email."**
    *   "I will. To be direct, this process is moving quickly. Is Tuesday or Wednesday better for a 15-minute call after you've seen the brief?"

### **SEGMENT 2: MEDIA BUSINESS BUYERS**
*Target:* Corp Dev at Media Companies, PE Firms, B2B Platforms

**15-Second Opener:**
"Hi [Name], Ewing from Next Chapter. We're representing the owner of **hr.com** — the largest HR media property globally. Are you the right person for potential acquisitions?"

**Value Prop (One Sentence):**
"HR.com delivers 1.8 million HR professionals, runs at a 20% EBITDA margin, and the founder is executing a transition after 27 years of building it."

**One Qualifying Question:**
"Is B2B media in professional services an area [Company] is actively looking to acquire in?"

**Meeting Set Close:**
"Let me send you the brief. Can we schedule 15 minutes this week to discuss the asset and the process?"

**Top 3 Objection Handles:**
1.  **"We're not looking at media."**
    *   "Fair. It's less about 'media' and more about owning a scaled, profitable audience in a $200B+ HR services market. It's a platform for any HR-adjacent service."
2.  **"What's the ask?"**
    *   "A formal process with a 60-day window. We're talking to strategic and financial buyers. The brief has the details."
3.  **"Send me info."**
    *   "I'll get the brief over now. To make the best use of your time, are you the sole decision-maker on something like this, or is there a partner/committee I should include?"

### **SEGMENT 3: MYPEOPLE INVESTORS**
*Target:* VC Partners, Growth Investors, HR Tech Acquirers

**15-Second Opener:**
"Hi [Name], Ewing from Next Chapter. I'm reaching out about an AI-native HR platform at $7M revenue looking for a growth partner and a CEO. Is that a fit for [Fund]?"

**Value Prop (One Sentence):**
"MyPeople.ai is an AI-native HR platform with a modern stack, 35 enterprise prospects in pipeline, built by the team behind HR.com, and it needs a growth CEO and capital."

**One Qualifying Question:**
"Is HR tech, specifically AI-driven platforms, within your current investment thesis?"

**Meeting Set Close:**
"Great. I'll send the deck and can introduce you to the interim CEO — he ran billion-dollar divisions at Oracle and SAP. What does your calendar look like later this week?"

**Top 3 Objection Handles:**
1.  **"We don't do search / we need a full team."**
    *   "The tech and product team are in place. This is a CEO search *plus* a growth capital round. We have the interim CEO locked in; you help us find the permanent one."
2.  **"Stage is too early / too late."**
    *   "It's a unique pivot. You're getting a de-risked, built platform with enterprise traction and a proven founding team, at a Series A entry point."
3.  **"Not enough traction."**
    *   "The traction is the 1.8 million-member HR.com audience as its built-in launch channel. The 35 enterprise prospects are a direct result of that. This is about scaling a proven model."
