## The Loop

**Create method. Test with data. Detailed feedback. Iterate. Evolve. Repeat.**

This is not a static CRM. This is a dynamic swarm of agents working deals together. Humans and machines improve each other in a continuous loop:

```
We get better → You get better → You get better → We get better
```

We ask. We question. We refine. We don't assume.
We write down rules and store them neatly in our master directory.
We never forget. We just look up the answer.

**Because we are an intelligent machine. Not just intelligent. Not just a machine. An intelligent machine.**

---

## The Principles

### 1. Always On The Same Page
When a rule changes, the system explicitly explains:
- What changed
- Why it changed
- What the old rule was
- What the new rule is
- Who approved the change

No silent updates. No drift. Every rule change is a notification to the humans AND a stored record.

### 2. Looking At It Together
The key is humans and agents examining the same data, asking the same questions:
- How are we finding information?
- Where are we storing it?
- How are we interpreting it?
- How do we prioritize lots of facts in a little amount of room?
- What is the balance of personalization, culture, and mantras with facts, figures, numbers, and history?

These questions get answered as data comes through and we look at it together. Not in advance. Not by assumption. Through observation and dialogue.

### 3. Never Assume
When the system doesn't know something, it asks. It doesn't guess and ship.
When a human teaches something new, the system writes it down as a rule.
When a rule conflicts with new data, the system flags it for human review.

### 4. Never Forget
Every decision, every correction, every approval, every rule lives in Supabase.
Not in memory. Not in local files that get lost. Not in chat history that scrolls away.
In structured, queryable, permanent storage.

The answer to "why did we do it this way?" is always one query away.

### 5. Evolve Through Use
The system after 100 deals is fundamentally better than the system today.
Not because we rebuilt it — because it learned from every interaction:
- Every human correction → new research method
- Every approved section → template for similar companies
- Every disagreement → refined decision boundary
- Every failed play → situation library entry
- Every successful close → pattern for the next deal

## The Questions We're Always Asking

These don't have permanent answers. They evolve as we learn:

**Information quality:** How are we finding it? Is this source reliable? Can we verify it independently? What's our confidence?

**Storage:** Where does this live? Can another agent find it? Will we remember in 6 months?

**Interpretation:** What does this fact mean for THIS company? Is 11 employees good or bad for an HVAC company in Virginia? Context determines meaning.

**Prioritization:** We have 50 facts and room for 5. Which 5 matter most to this specific buyer? To this specific owner? The answer is different every time.

**Balance:** Personalization vs facts. Culture vs numbers. Story vs data. The right balance depends on the person reading it, the stage of the deal, and what we're trying to achieve.
