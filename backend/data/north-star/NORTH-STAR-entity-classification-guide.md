# Section 5: Deterministic Classification Engine (Intent-First Routing)

**Step 3:** Is the company a hospital, medical device company, health-tech/wellness company, or energy asset?*If intent is known, skip the rest of the tree and assign immediately.*

**Core Principle**

Always classify based on PRIMARY INTENT, not industry alone. If multiple signals exist, use this priority order:

- **1.** Intent (What is happening?)

- **2.** Company Type (What are they?)

- **3.** Person Role (Who is involved?)

- **4.** Channel / Campaign (How did it originate?)

*Never classify based on folder location, tool, or data source.*

## Master Decision Tree

**Step 0:** Is there a CLEAR ACTION?

Ask: What is the goal of this interaction?

- Hiring → RevsUp

- Buying/selling a business → Next Chapter

- Investing / raising capital → AND Capital

- If YES → assign immediately. If NO → continue.

**Step 1:** Is this about HIRING or TALENT?

- Keywords: hiring, recruiting, headcount, open roles, candidates, placement

- **YES:** TAG: RevsUp

**Override rule:**

- Even if company = SaaS, if NOT about hiring → do NOT tag RevsUp

**Step 2:** Is this about BUYING or SELLING a BUSINESS?

- Keywords: sell my business, acquisition, buyer, seller, valuation, exit, roll-up

- **YES:** TAG: Next Chapter

Applies EVEN IF the company is large, the buyer is PE-backed, or the industry overlaps (e.g., healthcare services operator).

**Step 3:** Is this about INVESTING or CAPITAL?

Split into two sub-paths:

- **3A:** Raising or Allocating Capital --- LPs, family offices, RIAs, institutions → TAG: AND Capital (LP Side)

- **3B:** Investing INTO companies/assets --- Hospitals, energy, med-tech, etc. → TAG: AND Capital (Investment Side)

**Step 4:** Company-Type Fallback (only if intent is unclear)

- **4A:** SaaS / VC-backed tech company: If hiring → RevsUp. If NOT hiring → NO TAG (default).

- **4B:** Trades / Home Services → Next Chapter.

- **4C:** Healthcare / Energy / Assets → AND Capital.

- **4D:** Financial Entities (family office, RIA, fund) → AND Capital.

**Step 5:** M&A Intermediary Logic

If company = Investment Bank / Broker / Intermediary, ask:

- Are they sourcing deals for hospitals/energy? → AND Capital

- Are they sourcing trades businesses? → Next Chapter

- Are they irrelevant to both? → No Tag

**Step 6:** Multi-Signal Conflict Resolution

If multiple entities could apply, use this resolution table:

*Rule: ACTION beats INDUSTRY 100% of the time.*

- **SaaS company hiring sales team:** → RevsUp

- **SaaS company being acquired:** → NONE (not your focus)

- **Trades company raising capital:** → Next Chapter (NOT AND Capital)

- **Hospital hiring VP Sales:** → RevsUp (NOT AND Capital)

- **Family office buying HVAC company:** → Next Chapter (NOT AND Capital)

## Section 5.1: Fail-Safe Rules

These prevent bad tagging.

**Rule 1:** RevsUp ONLY exists if hiring is present. If no hiring, never tag RevsUp.

**Rule 2:** Next Chapter ONLY exists if a TRANSACTION is happening. If no buy/sell intent, do not tag Next Chapter.

**Rule 3:** AND Capital ONLY exists if CAPITAL is involved. If no investment or fundraising, do not tag AND Capital.

**Rule 4:** If NONE apply, assign NO TAG. Do NOT force classification.

## Confidence Scoring

Use this simple scoring model:

- Strong signal (explicit intent) = +2

- Moderate signal (industry match) = +1

- Weak signal (keyword only) = +0.5

**Tag only if score ≥ 2.**

## Edge Case Handling

**Edge Case 1:** Dual-role companies

Example: SaaS company also acquiring smaller companies.

- Hiring → RevsUp

- Acquisition → Not your system (ignore)

**Edge Case 2:** PE firms

- Raising capital → AND Capital

- Buying trades companies → Next Chapter

- Buying SaaS → ignore

**Edge Case 3:** "Healthcare services" businesses

- If OPERATING business being sold → Next Chapter

- If INVESTMENT asset → AND Capital

**Edge Case 4:** Contractors in "energy" or "medical"

- If contractor / service company → Next Chapter

- If asset / infrastructure → AND Capital
