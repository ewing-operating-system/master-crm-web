Strategic Assessment

The 100-Year Conversation: There are very few precast operators in the country that have been family-owned for five generations. That kind of longevity represents something the family is proud of — and that pride cuts both ways in an acquisition conversation. The Gillespies are not going to sell to a financial buyer who will strip the name off the building and integrate them into a roll-up. But they might partner with another family operator who understands what it means to build something that outlasts a single generation. Pat Fore III is that conversation partner. Design Precast is also a family business with a 40-year history. That is the opening. +30% Growth is an Urgency Signal: A 20-person operation growing at 30% annually is approaching an inflection point. Either they hire aggressively and take on the complexity of being a 30-40 person operation, or they bring in a partner who already has the infrastructure for that scale. This is the moment when family businesses are most receptive to conversations about partnership — not when they're struggling, but when they're succeeding and the next step feels daunting. Dry Utility is the Right Sector: Design Precast's core products are wastewater and stormwater — wet utility. Adding a dry utility partner gives the combined entity a complete underground infrastructure offering. Electric utilities, telecom companies, and fiber developers operate on different procurement cycles than DOT and municipal stormwater — which means dry utility revenue diversifies Design Precast's customer concentration. Mid-Atlantic Geographic Logic: Chestertown sits on Maryland's Eastern Shore, equidistant between Baltimore/DC metro and the Delmarva Peninsula. The customer base for dry utility vaults and manholes is concentrated utilities, municipalities, and contractors who operate across MD, DE, VA, and southern PA. This is a 4-state footprint from one plant. Acquisition Approach: Lead with the family-to-family narrative. Pat Fore III should make this call personally. The pitch is partnership and legacy preservation, not financial engineering. Offer a structure that keeps the Gillespie name on the plant if that matters to them.
