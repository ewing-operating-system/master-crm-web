Buyer Profile

American Water Works Company (NYSE: AWK) is the largest and only pure-play, large-cap water utility in the United States. The company reported 2024 full-year earnings of $5.39 per share (up from $4.90 in 2023), with weather-normalized and adjusted EPS of $5.18 (8.6% year-over-year growth). American Water invested $3.3 billion in regulated operations in 2024, led by infrastructure renewal, and completed 13 acquisitions adding nearly 70,000 customers, achieving its 2% annual acquisition growth target.

CEO M. Susan Hardwick announced her retirement effective May 2025, with President John Griffith assuming the CEO role. Griffith stated: "American Water once again successfully executed on its guidance and plan, including industry-leading earnings and dividend growth." The leadership transition signals continuity in American Water's aggressive acquisition strategy, which targets regulated water and wastewater systems across the U.S.

American Water's acquisition machine is highly relevant to AquaScience. The company has a stated target of 2% annual customer growth through acquisitions, and it completed 13 deals in 2024 alone. The company's approach focuses on acquiring smaller water and wastewater systems -- often municipal systems but also including treatment operations that complement its regulated utility footprint. American Water's scale ($3.3 billion annual capex) means it has the financial capacity to acquire and invest in any water asset that fits its strategic criteria.
