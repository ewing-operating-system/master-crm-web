Buyer Narrative

Forterra is one of the largest manufacturers of water and drainage pipe and precast concrete products in the United States, operating dozens of plants across the country. The company was taken private in 2024 through a major acquisition by Quikrete Holdings — itself a portfolio company of private equity firms — in a deal that valued Forterra at approximately $2.7 billion. Under Quikrete's ownership, Forterra has access to significant capital and a mandate to consolidate its position as the dominant infrastructure products platform in North America. Quikrete's acquisition of Forterra was driven by the thesis that aging U.S. water infrastructure, the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA), and accelerating municipal capital spending would create sustained demand for pipe and precast products over the next decade.

As a strategic acquirer, Forterra brings operational depth that financial buyers cannot match. They understand precast manufacturing, they run their own plants, and they know how to integrate acquired facilities into their logistics and distribution network. For a seller like Pat Fore III, this means Forterra isn't guessing about the business — they live it. They can evaluate equipment, workforce capability, and customer relationships with a practitioner's eye, which typically translates into fair valuations and smoother transitions. Post-Quikrete, Forterra has both the balance sheet and the strategic incentive to acquire complementary regional manufacturers that fill gaps in their geographic footprint.

Importantly, Forterra's leadership has publicly discussed growth through both organic expansion and acquisitions. Their interest is not speculative — they are actively building a coast-to-coast manufacturing network to serve the infrastructure boom. A Gulf Coast facility producing pipe and precast products is not a stretch for them; it is a direct extension of what they already do at scale.

Why This Seller

Design Precast & Pipe's Gulfport, Mississippi operation fills a clear geographic gap in Forterra's manufacturing network. Forterra has strong coverage in Texas, the Mid-Atlantic, and parts of the Southeast, but the Mississippi Gulf Coast — a region experiencing significant post-hurricane rebuilding, infrastructure modernization, and industrial expansion — represents an underserved market for their products. Design Precast's capabilities in both precast structures and pipe manufacturing align directly with Forterra's core product lines, meaning integration would be straightforward with minimal product-line overlap risk. The estimated $15M revenue size makes this a bolt-on acquisition that Forterra can absorb without integration complexity, while gaining an established workforce, customer base, and permitted facility in a high-growth corridor. Pat Fore III's decades of ownership mean the operation is stable and proven — exactly the type of platform Forterra prefers to acquire rather than build greenfield.
