Buyer Profile

Evoqua Water Technologies, now fully integrated into Xylem Inc. (NYSE: XYL), represents one of the most compelling strategic buyers for AquaScience. Xylem completed its landmark $7.5 billion all-stock acquisition of Evoqua in 2023, creating the world's largest pure-play water technology company with $7.3 billion in pro forma revenue and more than 22,000 employees globally. Headquartered in Washington, D.C., the combined entity operates across 150+ countries with a market capitalization surpassing $32 billion by late 2025.

Under CEO Matthew Pine and CFO Bill Grogan, Xylem delivered record full-year 2025 results: revenue of $9.0 billion (up 6% reported, 5% organically), adjusted EPS of $5.08 (up 19%), and Q4 orders of $2.4 billion (up 9%). The company initiated 2026 guidance of $9.1-$9.2 billion in revenue with adjusted EPS of $5.35-$5.60, signaling continued growth momentum. Keith Buettner serves as VP of Investor Relations and FP&A.

The strategic rationale for acquiring AquaScience is deeply aligned with Xylem's post-Evoqua integration thesis. Evoqua's legacy strength was in North American water treatment -- advanced water and wastewater treatment capabilities, a vast installed base of service contracts, and digital monitoring solutions. AquaScience's Wyoming-based water treatment operations would extend Xylem's geographic coverage in the Mountain West and Intermountain regions where water scarcity and treatment demand are intensifying.

Xylem's growth thesis centers on capturing the $401 billion water infrastructure market, leveraging 1,900+ patents and $1.2 billion in R&D investments to build smart water solutions for aging systems. The company has been actively pursuing an "80/20" strategic focus post-Evoqua integration, concentrating resources on highest-value customers and applications. AquaScience's specialized treatment capabilities would complement this approach.

Key M&A decision-makers include Tracy Evans, Director of Corporate Development at Xylem, and historically Amit Shah, who served as Sr. Director of Strategy and M&A at Evoqua. Karen Pulick continues as Director of Global IT and M&A Integration, indicating the organization maintains robust acquisition integration infrastructure. Given Xylem's demonstrated ability to integrate a $7.5 billion acquisition, a smaller tuck-in like AquaScience would be highly executable.

The fit is exceptionally strong: Xylem actively seeks water treatment assets that expand its service footprint, add recurring revenue streams, and deepen its technological capabilities in underserved geographies. AquaScience's Wyoming presence addresses a gap in Xylem's Mountain West coverage, and the company's treatment expertise aligns with Xylem's shift from equipment sales toward end-to-end water solutions.
