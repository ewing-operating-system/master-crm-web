Strategic Assessment

The Texas Thesis: Texas is not a market Design Precast currently serves — but the product lines are identical. Manholes, stormwater structures, duct banks, and utility vaults are the same specifications in Texas as in Mississippi and Louisiana. The difference is market size: Texas DOT (TxDOT) spends more annually on transportation infrastructure than most states' entire budgets. Houston alone has more linear feet of new utility infrastructure going in each year than all of Mississippi. StormCross as a Platform Asset: A proprietary product that gets specified into engineering drawings is worth meaningfully more than commodity precast revenue of the same dollar amount. When an engineer writes "StormCross Series 400" into a drainage plan, Locke Solutions is the only compliant supplier. Acquiring that IP and those engineering relationships acquires a recurring revenue stream that compounds as more projects get designed with StormCross specifications. Dual-Location Texas Footprint: Houston and Dallas together cover the two largest population centers in Texas. That is not a coincidence — someone built a multi-plant Texas network intentionally. The capital and organizational effort required to replicate that footprint organically would far exceed an acquisition premium. Metal Fab Cross-Sell: Design Precast's current product line is concrete-only. Adding a metal fabrication capability — even as a secondary line — opens utility vault covers, manhole frames, grating, and ladder systems as integrated offerings. Contractors who currently source concrete from Design Precast and metal from a different vendor could consolidate to a single supplier. Risk Factors: Texas is a new geography — Design Precast has no existing customer relationships there. Key-man risk on StormCross engineering relationships must be assessed. Confirm TxDOT prequalification status. Texas union vs. non-union labor dynamics differ from Gulf Coast norms.
