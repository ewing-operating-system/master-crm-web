Strategic Assessment

The Certification Moat: Florence Concrete Products holds active fabricator approvals from four state DOTs simultaneously. FDOT alone is one of the most restrictive and coveted approvals in southeastern precast — the application process, plant audits, and qualification runs take 12–24 months and a meaningful capital outlay. Acquiring a business that already holds FDOT + GDOT + VDOT + SCDOT certifications is acquiring a 4-state DOT bidding license. That is a strategic asset independent of any revenue multiple. Geographic Fit for Design Precast: Design Precast operates primarily in Mississippi, Louisiana, and the adjacent Gulf Coast. Florence, SC positions the combined entity to bid on DOT work from Florida to Virginia — a corridor with over $20B annually in state transportation spending. The Carolinas and Virginia are underserved by Gulf Coast operators. Prestressed vs. Standard Precast: Prestressed bridge fabrication is a step up in capability from standard precast pipe and utility structures. The labor, quality control, and equipment requirements are more demanding. Evaluate whether Design Precast's leadership team wants to operate in this segment or whether the DOT relationships alone (for non-bridge products) are the value to extract. 70-Year History as a Signal: A company that has operated continuously for 70 years in a capital-intensive, relationship-driven business has survived multiple recessions, material cycles, and DOT procurement changes. The operational durability is real. The exit risk is succession — not business quality. Due Diligence Priorities: (1) DOT certification transferability upon change of control. (2) Age and condition of prestressing beds and forms. (3) Backlog and pipeline of DOT contracts. (4) Key-man risk — how much of the DOT relationships sit with the owner personally.
