Strategic Fit

Foley Products is one of the most significant precast and RCP operations in the Southeast. Their plant network in Alabama, Tennessee, and South Carolina maps almost perfectly onto the geographic expansion corridor Design Precast would need to become a true regional player. The pipe and precast overlap is exact — these are the same product lines Pat Fore already runs in Biloxi.

Commercial Metals Company is primarily a steel and rebar business. The acquisition of Foley almost certainly had a strategic rationale tied to rebar-to-finished-concrete integration — but managing multiple precast plants across four states is operationally distant from CMC's core. History shows that when industrial conglomerates absorb family-owned concrete operations, non-core plants get rationalized within 18–36 months. At least one Alabama or Tennessee plant is a plausible divestiture candidate within that window.

Bob Palmer ran operations at Foley/Old Castle for 20 years before joining Georgia Concrete & Precast. He has direct knowledge of plant performance, customer relationships, and which facilities are underperformers. He is a warm contact (Dec 2025 call) and has agreed to flag buy/sell opportunities — he should be the first call when CMC integration news emerges.

Approach Strategy

Call Opener: "Bob, Pat Fore here in Biloxi. I've been watching the CMC-Foley integration closely — you're my best source for what's actually happening on the ground. Any signals yet on which plants they're keeping versus rationalizing?"

1
Lead through Bob Palmer, not CMC directly. Bob has plant-level intelligence CMC press releases will never reveal. Ask specifically about the Alabama and Tennessee plants — those are the most likely non-core assets given CMC's steel distribution network in the Southeast.
2
Position Design Precast as the preferred strategic buyer, not a financial acquirer. When CMC decides to divest, they will have choices. A Gulf Coast operator who keeps plants running, keeps workers employed, and knows the DOT bid market is a more credible steward than a fund. That story needs to be planted early — before any process starts.
3
Monitor WARN Act filings, CMC earnings calls, and trade press for integration signals. Set Google Alerts for "Foley Products" + "CMC" + "divestiture." Flag any plant closure or workforce reduction — those are the entry points. Re-evaluate for direct CMC outreach in Q3 2026 after integration clarity expected.
