Stanley Steemer International is the largest carpet and floor cleaning company in the United States. Founded in 1947 in Columbus, Ohio by Jack Bauer, the company remains family-owned and operated under the Bauer family across generations. Stanley Steemer pioneered the hot water extraction cleaning method and built its reputation on the strength of its 1-800-STEEMER brand, which is among the most recognized service brands in American households.

The company operates through a hybrid model: a combination of company-owned territories and an extensive franchise network of 300-plus franchisees. Stanley Steemer manufactures its own cleaning equipment and trucks in-house, training all technicians to a proprietary standard. Core services include carpet cleaning, hard surface floor cleaning, upholstery cleaning, air duct cleaning, water damage restoration, and mold remediation.

Stanley Steemer has expanded aggressively through franchise territory acquisitions in recent years. In February 2025, Stanley Steemer of South Florida acquired the Miami-Dade and Broward County operations from Stanley Steemer International, becoming the largest franchise in the system by sales volume. This pattern of territory consolidation and market densification reflects the company's broader growth strategy: tightening its presence in underpenetrated regional markets rather than entering entirely new verticals.

Iowa represents one of the least saturated markets in the Stanley Steemer footprint. The Midwest corridor — particularly Des Moines and its surrounding metro — presents a logical next step for either a corporate territory buildout or a franchisee expansion. Springer Floor Care's existing customer relationships, trained workforce, and brand presence in that market make it a turnkey entry point.

Strategic Fit

Iowa market entry with zero ramp time. Stanley Steemer has limited franchise presence in Iowa. Springer Floor Care operates a functioning, revenue-generating floor care business in Des Moines with an established customer base and local brand recognition. An acquisition means Stanley Steemer inherits existing routes, repeat customers, and community goodwill — not a greenfield startup risk.

Eddie Holman's IICRC credentials are a real asset. Eddie holds Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification (IICRC) credentials — the industry's gold standard. Stanley Steemer mandates IICRC-certified technicians across its network. Eddie's certification status signals that Springer Floor already operates to Stanley Steemer's quality standard, reducing integration friction and training costs. In a transition arrangement, Eddie could serve as a training resource or market manager during handoff.

Forever Kleen product line has integration upside. Springer Floor's proprietary Forever Kleen product line represents either a co-marketing opportunity or a product acquisition play. Stanley Steemer has historically invested in proprietary cleaning solutions and may see value in a regional brand with demonstrated customer loyalty. At minimum, the existing product relationships and supply chain remove a procurement headache from day one.

Terri and Eddie are retirement-motivated — clean exit, no drama. The Holmans (Terri, 68; Eddie, 70) are ready to transition. They are not looking to stay on indefinitely or negotiate for equity rollovers. This is a clean, motivated sell — exactly the profile that makes integration straightforward for an acquirer that wants to move quickly and efficiently.
