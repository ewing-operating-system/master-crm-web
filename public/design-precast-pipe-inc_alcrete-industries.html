Strategic Fit

Alcrete was founded in 2020, which means the founders are now five years in with a two-plant operation. That is the classic inflection point: keep grinding with personal capital, take on debt to grow, or find a partner with infrastructure and scale. Design Precast is exactly that partner — an established operator who can offer operational credibility, shared purchasing power on raw materials, and a path for the Alcrete founders to take something off the table while staying involved if they choose.

The Pell City plant sits in a high-traffic DOT corridor between Birmingham and Atlanta. Design Precast currently operates from Biloxi — an Alabama plant extends their reach north and east into projects they cannot currently compete on due to freight cost. This is not geography for geography's sake; it is competitive positioning on specific ALDOT bid lists.

The Fort Pierce plant is a bonus — Florida infrastructure spend is substantial and growing. If the Alabama deal makes sense, Florida comes along. If the founders want to retain Florida separately, that is a negotiating point, not a dealbreaker. Product overlap with Design Precast is exact on RCP and box culverts. The detention product line adds adjacent capability Design Precast could absorb or cross-sell.

Approach Strategy

Call Opener: "My name is Pat Fore — I run Design Precast & Pipe out of Biloxi. I've been watching what you've built at Alcrete and I think there's a real conversation to be had about what the next five years looks like for both of us."

1
Lead with peer respect, not acquisition intent. Alcrete built a real two-plant RCP and box culvert operation from scratch in five years. That takes grit and capital. Open by acknowledging what they built — not by making an offer. "Joining forces creates value for both entities" is the right entry before any valuation talk.
2
Ask about their five-year vision before pitching Design Precast's. The founders may want to grow aggressively, slow down, or something in between. Understanding their goals — growth capital, reduced personal guarantee exposure, succession — shapes every subsequent conversation. Ask what they are trying to build, not what they are trying to sell.
3
Frame Alabama as the primary strategic prize, Florida as optionality. The Pell City-to-Birmingham corridor is the geographic argument Design Precast needs. If the conversation goes well, position a combined entity as the dominant Gulf-to-Alabama RCP operator with Florida optionality — a much stronger market position than either company holds alone.
