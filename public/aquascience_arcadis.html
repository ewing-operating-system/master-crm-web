Buyer Profile

Arcadis NV (Euronext: ARCAD) is a leading global design and consultancy firm for natural and built assets, with significant operations in water, environment, and infrastructure. The company reported 2025 full-year net revenues of EUR 3.8 billion, with organic growth of -0.5% but total backlog of EUR 3.6 billion showing organic growth. Free cash flow was EUR 288 million (up 26.3%), and operating EBITA was EUR 418 million with an 11.1% margin (down from 11.5% in 2024).

Under outgoing CEO Alan Brookes and incoming CEO nominee Heather Polinsky, Arcadis is navigating a strategic transformation. The Q4 2025 results showed mixed performance: strong performers in Mobility (UK and Australia), Property and Investment, and Resilience were offset by some areas of weakness. Critically for AquaScience, Arcadis's Environmental Restoration, Water Optimization, Energy Transition, and Climate Adaptation segments represent core growth priorities.

Arcadis secured two contracts in Los Angeles worth over $18 million over 5 years for project management and construction management in March 2026, demonstrating continued U.S. market investment. The company's CFO Simon Crowe oversees financial strategy including M&A capital allocation. Arcadis operates globally but has particular strength in North America, where it has been expanding its environmental and water consulting capabilities.

The strategic rationale for Arcadis acquiring AquaScience lies in Arcadis's Water Optimization practice. While Arcadis is primarily a consulting and design firm, the company has been moving toward more integrated solutions that combine advisory with operational capabilities. AquaScience's hands-on water treatment operations in Wyoming would give Arcadis a captive operational platform to complement its consulting services in the Mountain West region.

AquaScience's fit with Arcadis is particularly compelling in the context of Arcadis's Resilience segment, which focuses on climate adaptation and environmental restoration -- areas where water treatment is increasingly critical. Wyoming's energy, mining, and agricultural sectors all generate complex water treatment needs that align with Arcadis's expertise in environmental remediation and sustainable water management.
