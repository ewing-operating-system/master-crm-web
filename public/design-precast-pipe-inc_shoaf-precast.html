Strategic Assessment

The 60-Year Inflection: Shoaf Precast was founded in 1966. The founding generation is gone. The second generation is at or past retirement age. The third generation — if there is one active in the business — is making the decision that every family business faces: carry it forward or find a good home for it. Design Precast's 40-year story is the most credible voice in that conversation. Carolinas Foothold: The Carolinas are one of the strongest residential and light-commercial construction markets in the Southeast. Charlotte and Raleigh metros continue to grow at rates that drive septic system demand in surrounding counties, municipal drainage upgrades in growing jurisdictions, and commercial site work in every exurban corridor. Shoaf's 60-year network in this market is not replicable. Septic as a Channel Extension: Design Precast does not currently serve the septic market. Acquiring Shoaf adds a residential/light commercial product line that runs on the same plant equipment. The septic installer network is a distinct channel from DOT contractors — it diversifies Design Precast's customer base in a way that reduces DOT bid-cycle exposure. Score Rationale (6): The septic focus creates partial product overlap rather than full alignment. Design Precast's core is DOT/municipal pipe and structures — Shoaf's core is residential septic. The structures line bridges the gap, but this is not a pure bolt-on the way SE Pipe & Precast (score 7) would be. That said, 60 years of Carolinas market presence is a hard asset. The right conversation could reveal structures revenue that moves this score upward. Outreach Approach: Lead with legacy and the shared experience of running a family-owned precast business for multiple generations. Do not lead with the product gap. Establish trust first; product line fit is a diligence conversation, not an opening.
