Strategic Fit

Corvus operates commercial janitorial franchises across 24 US cities with 226+ franchise units. Floor care is a natural add-on service within their existing customer relationships — facility managers who already use Corvus for janitorial would logically consolidate floor cleaning under the same vendor.

Springer Floor's commercial account base in the Kansas City market would represent an immediate revenue add for any Corvus franchisee in the region, and the trained workforce and equipment base transfers cleanly into their model.

The primary constraint is financial: thin franchise unit economics (~$110K–$175K AUV) and no PE backing mean Corvus lacks the capital to execute a meaningful acquisition without outside financing. This is a strategic fit story without a credible funding mechanism today.

Approach Strategy

Call Opener: "We work with a commercial floor care company in Kansas City that already serves the type of clients your franchisees clean every day. I wanted to have a quick conversation about whether there's a way to deepen your service offering in that market — either through an acquisition or a preferred subcontractor arrangement."
1
Corvus franchisees already have the facility manager relationships — Springer Floor can plug in as the floor care specialist without adding sales cost, immediately expanding revenue per account.
2
The Kansas City market is a natural fit: Springer Floor's commercial client roster maps directly to the commercial buildings Corvus franchisees service.
3
Even if an outright acquisition isn't feasible, a preferred subcontractor arrangement could be structured to test the model with minimal capital commitment — and position Corvus for a future bolt-on once PE capital is in place.
