Strategic Fit

Design Precast is currently a Gulf Coast operator. Texas is the natural westward extension — it shares the same climate, the same DOT bidding culture, and the same customer types (municipal, commercial, infrastructure contractors). Moving into Texas is not a geographic leap; it is a lane extension on the same highway. AmeriTex is a direct analog to Design Precast operating in the Texas market — the same business, different geography.

Two established plants, 17 years of operational history, TxDOT-approved products, and a focus on exactly the product lines Design Precast already manufactures. The Seguin plant covers San Antonio, the Hill Country, and I-10 west; the Conroe plant covers Houston, the Woodlands, and I-45 north toward Dallas. Together they reach three of the top-five Texas metro markets. This is not a fixer-upper — it is a clean bolt-on operating in the highest-spending DOT state in the country.

At 17 years, the founders of AmeriTex have built real equity value. They are past the survival phase and likely past the aggressive growth phase. The question they are probably asking themselves is what the next ten years looks like — whether that means continuing to run two plants indefinitely or finding a structure that lets them access the value they've built. Design Precast — with its own 40-year track record and a founder who understands that exact question — is a credible conversation partner.

Approach Strategy

Call Opener: "My name is Pat Fore — I run Design Precast & Pipe out of Biloxi. We've been in the RCP and box culvert business for 40 years. I've been watching what TxDOT is putting out and I think there's a real case for a Gulf Coast to Texas operator combination. I'd like to have a conversation about what you're building."

1
Lead with the Texas infrastructure spending environment. TxDOT's 10-year Unified Transportation Program exceeds $100B. That is a natural conversation opener that demonstrates industry knowledge and establishes Pat Fore as a peer who tracks the same market dynamics — not a financial buyer who looked up AmeriTex on a list.
2
Ask about their vision for the Seguin and Conroe plants before any deal discussion. Position as an operator looking to grow Texas presence through partnership. Ask what they are trying to build in the next five years — if the founders are ready to access liquidity, they will signal it. If they want to keep running, that conversation needs to happen before it becomes an acquisition conversation.
3
Emphasize operational fit, not financial engineering. Design Precast is a 40-year family operator — not a fund. The pitch to AmeriTex is that combining their Texas market position with Design Precast's Gulf Coast infrastructure creates a multi-state operator with no redundant overhead and identical product lines. Both founders stay relevant. No one gets absorbed into a corporate structure.
