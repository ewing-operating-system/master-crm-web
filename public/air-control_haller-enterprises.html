Strategic Fit

Haller Enterprises was built over 40+ years by Rick Haller from a single truck in a Lancaster County garage into a 200-truck, five-branch operation — a founder story with direct parallels to Air Control's trajectory in Northern Virginia. Both companies were built the same way: slowly, with a focus on people and repeat customer relationships. That shared origin creates an unusually natural cultural alignment between buyer and seller.

Haller won ACCA's Commercial Contractor of the Year award for all of south-central Pennsylvania — their commercial HVAC playbook is proven. Air Control would let them apply that same playbook in the DC metro, which is arguably the densest federal and commercial HVAC market on the East Coast and a geography Haller has not yet entered.

Kenny Haller's rise from within — 24 years of service before taking the president role — signals that Haller values continuity and operator tenure. For Fred Lowry, who cares deeply about what happens to his team post-close, a buyer that promotes from within rather than installs outside management is a meaningfully different conversation than a financial platform rollup.
