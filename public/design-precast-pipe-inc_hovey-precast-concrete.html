Strategic Fit

Opelika is 200 miles northeast of Biloxi and sits at the heart of the Auburn–Columbus growth corridor. The Auburn University campus generates steady infrastructure demand; Lee County is one of Alabama's fastest-growing counties. A plant here gives Design Precast reach into a market that is not currently served from Biloxi — and access to ALDOT bid lists in the eastern Alabama corridor.

Hovey's design-build positioning is a capability Design Precast can absorb and deploy system-wide. Custom engineered precast commands higher margins than standard catalog items and opens DOT design-assist contracts that commodity producers cannot bid. This is a capability premium, not just a geographic addition.

If Hovey does not manufacture pipe (likely), Design Precast's pipe capability becomes an immediate value-add post-acquisition — the combined entity can bid pipe-and-precast packages that Hovey currently cannot win alone. Score 6 vs. 7 reflects unconfirmed revenue scale and the absence of pipe manufacturing. If Hovey has pipe, this moves to a 7. If revenue confirms at a meaningful scale, same result.

Approach Strategy

Call Opener: "I'm Pat Fore — I run Design Precast & Pipe out of Biloxi, a 40-year family business in RCP and precast. We're looking to grow with an operator who has the same mentality — not selling to a fund, joining forces with another builder. I'd like to talk about what you're doing in Opelika."

1
Lead with "joining forces," not "acquisition." Design Precast is a family operator, not a financial buyer. The message should be: "We're both builders. We're looking for a partner who wants to keep running their business with more resources behind them." That framing invites a conversation rather than a defensive response.
2
Ask about their pipe supply chain. If Hovey currently sources pipe externally, Design Precast's manufacturing capability is an immediate cost-reduction opportunity the owner can see clearly. Ask where they buy pipe now and what that relationship looks like — this naturally surfaces the value Design Precast would bring without requiring a valuation discussion.
3
Reference the Auburn-Opelika growth corridor as a market tailwind. Opelika is in one of Alabama's fastest-growing counties. An owner who built a business in that market understands the opportunity. Connecting Design Precast's interest to the specific infrastructure demand in Lee County and the Columbus GA corridor shows market homework and positions this as a growth conversation, not a distressed exit.
