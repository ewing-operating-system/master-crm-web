Buyer Narrative

Commercial Metals Company (CMC) is a publicly traded, Fortune 500 industrial manufacturer headquartered in Irving, Texas, with over $7 billion in annual revenue and operations spanning North America, Europe, and Asia. CMC has historically been known as one of the largest steel and rebar producers in the United States, but in recent years the company has made a decisive strategic pivot into precast concrete — signaling a major platform build. Their December 2025 acquisition of Concrete Pipe & Precast (CP&P) for $675 million was a landmark deal that instantly made CMC one of the largest precast concrete operators in the country, and the subsequent acquisition of Foley Products further cemented their commitment to building a national precast platform.

What makes CMC a compelling buyer for a family-owned precast operation is their track record of acquiring well-run regional businesses and integrating them thoughtfully rather than stripping them down. CMC has the financial muscle of a $5B+ market cap public company, but their precast strategy is built on acquiring strong regional operators with established customer relationships and local manufacturing expertise — exactly the kind of business Wieser represents. Peter Matt, as a key executive in CMC's growth strategy, understands the value of regional reputation and operational excellence.

For a seller evaluating CMC, the key consideration is stewardship. CMC isn't a financial buyer looking to flip — they're building a long-term infrastructure platform. They have the capital to invest in equipment, the operational expertise to support growth, and the strategic vision to position acquired companies within a larger national footprint. Employees, customers, and community relationships tend to be preserved because CMC needs those assets to function within their platform model.

Why This Seller

Wieser Concrete Products is a near-perfect geographic and strategic fit for CMC's precast platform build. CMC's CP&P and Foley acquisitions gave them strong positions in the South and Southeast, but they have a clear gap in Upper Midwest manufacturing capacity. Wieser's Maiden Rock, Wisconsin operation fills that gap precisely — providing dedicated manufacturing infrastructure, established distribution relationships, and decades of regional expertise across Wisconsin, Minnesota, Iowa, and the Dakotas. Wieser's estimated $30-64M revenue represents the kind of bolt-on scale that CMC has shown it can integrate effectively, and the specialized precast products Wieser manufactures complement rather than overlap with CMC's existing portfolio. For Andy Wieser, CMC offers the rare combination of a buyer with the resources to grow the business and the strategic motivation to keep the Wisconsin operation running and investing long-term.
