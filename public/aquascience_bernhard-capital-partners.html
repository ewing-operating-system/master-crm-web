Strategic Fit

Bernhard Capital Partners has already placed its conviction in water infrastructure through National Water Infrastructure — one of Louisiana's largest private wastewater utility operators. That bet demonstrates BCP isn't exploring water; they're operating in it. AquaScience fits either as a complement to NWI's geographic expansion or as a standalone Western U.S. water treatment platform seed.

BCP's stated investment thesis explicitly calls out water and wastewater utilities as target sectors. More importantly, their approach — reducing costs, improving capital plan execution, increasing reliability, introducing innovative technology to modernize critical systems — maps precisely to what a Wyoming water treatment operator needs post-acquisition to grow from regional to regional-plus.

The Mountain West geography is also strategically differentiated from BCP's current Louisiana concentration. A Wyoming water treatment platform diversifies BCP's infrastructure book by both geography and end-market (industrial/energy customers vs. residential wastewater). That portfolio logic makes AquaScience a natural next move for BCP after NWI.
