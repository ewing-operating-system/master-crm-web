Your Company Story

Design Precast & Pipe, Inc. is a specialized manufacturer that has carved out a formidable position in one of construction's most essential — and most difficult to replicate — niches. Founded in 2004 in Gulfport, Mississippi, the company has spent over two decades building the kind of operational capability that only comes from years of solving hard problems in precast concrete and pipe manufacturing. With an estimated 52 employees producing an estimated $15 million in annual revenue, Design Precast operates at a scale that commands serious attention from infrastructure buyers — large enough to handle major projects, focused enough to maintain the quality control and customer relationships that define best-in-class specialty manufacturers. Gulfport's position on the Gulf Coast is not incidental to the company's success — it is central to it. The Gulf Coast corridor from Texas to Florida represents one of the fastest-growing infrastructure markets in the United States, driven by population growth, hurricane resilience mandates, and massive public and private investment in water, wastewater, and transportation infrastructure. Design Precast sits at the geographic center of this demand, with logistics advantages that distant competitors simply cannot match. Precast concrete is heavy, expensive to ship, and time-sensitive — which means proximity to the job site is a structural competitive advantage that no amount of marketing or pricing can overcome. At approximately $288,000 in revenue per employee, Design Precast demonstrates the operational discipline characteristic of a well-managed manufacturing operation. The company's 4.0 Google rating across 25 reviews reflects consistent delivery quality — notable in an industry where on-time, on-spec performance is the difference between repeat business and replacement. Over 22 years of continuous operation, Design Precast has built something that matters enormously to acquirers: institutional knowledge of Gulf Coast soil conditions, municipal specifications, DOT requirements, and contractor relationships that took decades to accumulate and cannot be replicated by simply opening a new plant.

Your Market Landscape

The precast concrete and pipe manufacturing sector is experiencing a historic convergence of demand drivers that has made it one of the most actively pursued verticals in building products M&A. The $1.2 trillion Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) is driving unprecedented federal spending on water systems, bridges, highways, and stormwater infrastructure — all of which require precast concrete components. Municipal water and wastewater systems across the Gulf Coast are under federal mandate to upgrade aging infrastructure, creating a multi-decade demand cycle that is largely recession-resistant because it is driven by regulatory compliance rather than discretionary spending. Private equity platforms have taken notice. Building products and specialty construction materials companies are trading at 3.0x–6.0x EBITDA for private businesses, with premiums for companies that have geographic exclusivity, municipal relationships, and diversified product lines. Major acquirers in this space include Quikrete Holdings (acquired Forterra's pipe and precast division), RINKER (Summit Materials), and regional platforms backed by firms like Clearlake Capital, KKR, and Platinum Equity. Several PE-backed building products platforms are actively pursuing Gulf Coast acquisitions to capture IIJA-funded project flow. The supply side is equally favorable for sellers. Precast manufacturing requires significant capital investment in forms, batch plants, curing facilities, and specialized equipment — creating high barriers to entry that protect existing operators. Permitting a new precast plant in a coastal zone takes 18–36 months, and skilled labor in precast manufacturing is scarce. These dynamics mean that acquirers who want Gulf Coast capacity must buy it — they cannot build it fast enough to capture the current infrastructure spending wave.
