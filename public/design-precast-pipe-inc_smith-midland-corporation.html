Strategic Assessment

The Public Company Opportunity: Small OTC-traded companies in capital-intensive manufacturing industries are frequently undervalued relative to private comparables. The public structure creates overhead (audit costs, disclosure obligations, board requirements) that a $10–30M revenue manufacturing business struggles to justify. Many small public companies would prefer to go private — but taking a company private requires a buyer. Design Precast + Next Chapter could be that buyer. Audited Financials are a Gift: Every precast acquisition requires financial due diligence. With SMID, the top-line revenue, gross margin, SG&A, and debt structure are available in public filings before the first conversation. This compresses the discovery phase significantly and lets Design Precast walk into the first call already knowing the business. Look up SMID on OTC Markets and read the last two annual reports before outreach. Sound Wall as a Capability Acquisition: SlenderWall and the sound wall product line give Design Precast access to FHWA noise abatement project bidding — a category that requires specific acoustic testing, FHWA approval, and production tooling that most precast operators simply don't have. Every interstate expansion, HOV lane addition, and highway widening project near a residential area requires noise walls. This is a durable DOT product category. J-J Hooks Licensing Revenue: The J-J Hooks highway barrier system is licensed to other manufacturers. Acquiring SMID acquires that licensing royalty stream. It also means Design Precast could license the system into their Gulf Coast market, where highway barrier demand is significant. 3-Plant Network Value: The standalone value of three operational precast plants — buildings, equipment, forms, stressing equipment, yard infrastructure — likely exceeds the public market capitalization of SMID at small-cap OTC valuations. The fixed asset value provides a floor; the revenue, certifications, and IP provide the upside. Acquisition Structure: OTC public company acquisitions can be structured as a tender offer (for all outstanding shares) or a negotiated merger with board approval. Given SMID's small float and likely founder/family concentration, a negotiated deal with the controlling family is the most efficient path. M&A counsel with public company experience is required. Geographic Fit: SMID's three plants (VA, NC, GA) are all east of Design Precast's current footprint (MS, LA). Together, the combined entity would operate from the Gulf Coast to the Mid-Atlantic — a 7-state Southeast corridor with no geographic gaps.
