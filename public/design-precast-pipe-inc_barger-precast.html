Strategic Assessment

Why 1945 Matters: C.R. Barger & Sons has been operating for over 80 years. That is not a coincidence — it represents deep customer loyalty, TDOT approval status built over decades, and a workforce that knows how to make quality product. It also means the current generation of ownership is almost certainly navigating one of the most common family business challenges: what happens to an 80-year-old company when the next generation either doesn't want to run it, or wants liquidity without losing the legacy. The Stone Strong Angle: Most precast producers in the Southeast make manholes, pipe, and box culverts — that is the commodity core. Barger's Stone Strong license is different. Stone Strong blocks are used in large-scale retaining wall applications — highway embankments, commercial site work, erosion control. It is a higher-value, more defensible product line. Any acquirer inherits that license relationship, which is worth understanding in detail before any offer discussion. Tennessee Footprint: Design Precast currently operates from Biloxi. Tennessee extends the footprint northward into a fast-developing infrastructure market. The I-75/I-40 interchange at Lenoir City is one of the most active construction corridors in the Southeast due to ongoing TDOT expansion, Toyota's Huntsville plant draw, and the broader East Tennessee development wave. Approach: Lead with respect for 80 years of operation. Pat Fore's own 40-year story in Biloxi is the credibility marker — two families who built real businesses and believe in what they built. The conversation is about what the Barger family wants the next chapter to look like, and whether Design Precast is the right partner to make that happen on their terms.
