Strategic Fit

Mobile sits directly between Design Precast's Gulfport/Biloxi base and the Alabama/Florida panhandle market. This is a contiguous geographic fill — not a geographic reach. A small precast operation in Mobile is the cleanest possible tuck-in acquisition: it extends service radius 60 miles east, captures the Mobile Bay construction market, and creates a stepping stone toward the Pensacola panhandle without requiring a large capital commitment.

Small size is a feature, not a limitation. At this scale, the owner is likely running the plant directly. That means a relatively simple ownership conversation with one decision-maker, no board, no investment committee, and no advisor intermediary. It also means the owner has probably not had a serious succession conversation — Design Precast can be the first credible buyer they have ever talked to.

The geographic gap between Gulfport and Pensacola is Design Precast's most obvious competitive blind spot. Any DOT or municipal project in the Mobile-to-Pensacola corridor currently requires long freight hauls from Biloxi. A Mobile plant closes that gap and makes Design Precast competitive on bids they currently cannot win on freight economics alone.

Approach Strategy

Call Opener: "I'm Pat Fore — I run Design Precast & Pipe out of Biloxi, about an hour west of you. We've been in precast and pipe for 40 years. I'm reaching out to operators in your area because we're looking to grow eastward, and I'd like to have a conversation about what you've built."

1
Open as a neighbor, not a buyer. Pat Fore is literally 60 miles away. The proximity is an asset — this is not a distant acquirer making an unsolicited call. It is a neighboring operator who serves the same infrastructure contractors and understands the same market. Lead with that shared context before any mention of acquisition.
2
Ask about their customer base and which projects they bid. Understanding whether Coastal Precast serves ALDOT directly, county public works, or commercial developers shapes the acquisition rationale. If they have established ALDOT vendor relationships, those approvals have real value. Learn what they have before framing what Design Precast brings.
3
Tuck-in framing, not acquisition framing. The message to a small operator is not "we want to buy your company." It is "we want to grow the business together — you keep running it, we back it with 40 years of purchasing power and customer relationships." That framing is far more likely to get a second conversation than a direct acquisition approach.
