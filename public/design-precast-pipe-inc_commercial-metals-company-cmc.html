Buyer Narrative

Commercial Metals Company (CMC) is a Fortune 500 steel and building materials manufacturer headquartered in Irving, Texas, with over $8 billion in annual revenue and operations spanning North America, Europe, and Asia. Founded in 1915, CMC has evolved from a scrap metal dealer into one of the largest vertically integrated steel companies in the United States, operating electric arc furnace mini-mills, steel fabrication plants, and — increasingly — precast concrete manufacturing facilities. Their strategic pivot into precast concrete has been aggressive and deliberate, signaling a long-term commitment to infrastructure-adjacent building products.

CMC's precast platform strategy crystallized with the $675 million acquisition of CP&P (formerly Central Pre-Mix Prestress), which gave them dominant positioning across the Mid-Atlantic and parts of the Southeast. They followed that with the acquisition of Foley Products in 2025, extending their reach deeper into the Southeast with plants in Alabama and Georgia. Together, these moves have made CMC arguably the largest precast concrete platform in the country — a position they appear intent on defending and expanding. Peter Matt, who leads corporate development efforts in this vertical, has been the point person on these transactions.

For a seller like Pat Fore, CMC represents the rare buyer that combines Fortune 500 financial resources with genuine operational understanding of heavy building materials. They are not a financial sponsor looking to flip — they are a strategic acquirer building a national platform, which means they value operational continuity, local management expertise, and long-term asset positioning. Sellers who join the CMC platform retain relevance within a much larger organization while gaining access to capital, procurement leverage, and national distribution that no independent operator can match.

Why This Seller

Design Precast & Pipe's Gulfport, Mississippi operation sits in the most obvious geographic gap in CMC's emerging national precast platform. CMC has locked down the Mid-Atlantic through CP&P and the Southeast through Foley Products, and they have legacy Texas operations — but the Gulf Coast corridor from Mississippi through Louisiana remains uncovered. Design Precast bridges that gap perfectly, giving CMC distribution reach into Gulf Coast infrastructure projects, Mississippi DOT work, and the broader I-10 corridor without requiring greenfield investment. Pat Fore's established relationships with local contractors, municipalities, and DOTs are exactly the kind of embedded market position that CMC cannot replicate by simply opening a new plant. At an estimated $15M in revenue, this is a clean bolt-on that slots directly into CMC's platform thesis without integration complexity.
