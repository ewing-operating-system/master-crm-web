Strategic Fit

Harvard Maintenance is a 60-year-old commercial cleaning and facility services company operating at approximately $700M in revenue. They serve Class A office buildings, mixed-use properties, luxury hotels, and institutional clients — the same category of commercial facility managers that Springer Floor's B2B accounts include. Harvard has a dedicated corporate development function and a confirmed acquisition on record (Pro Clean, reported by ISSA).

Floor care is a natural service extension within Harvard's existing commercial cleaning scope. Their JLL-referred case study shows they regularly enter multi-service relationships with Class A properties where floor maintenance is a component of the overall facility services contract. Acquiring a specialized floor care operator would allow Harvard to bring that service line in-house, improve margins on floor care line items, and potentially expand floor care offerings across their national commercial account base.

The geographic mismatch is the primary risk: Harvard is headquartered in New York and operationally concentrated in the Northeast and Southeast. Springer Floor operates in the Kansas City/Midwest market. This is an acquirable gap only if Harvard has Midwest expansion ambitions or sees the operational model as worth deploying in new markets.

Approach Strategy

Call Opener: "Harvard does the janitorial work in Class A commercial properties — we represent a floor care specialist that handles the hard-surface maintenance in exactly those buildings. I'd like to have a quick conversation about whether bringing that capability in-house makes sense as part of your expansion."
1
Harvard already operates in multi-service commercial facility contracts where floor care is a line item. Acquiring Springer Floor converts that subcontracted cost into a captive margin — and gives Harvard a proprietary floor care offering to lead with in new account pitches.
2
Harvard's corporate development team is active (Pro Clean acquisition confirmed) — this is a company with the organizational appetite and infrastructure for acquisitions, not just organic hiring.
3
The Midwest market represents white space for Harvard's commercial cleaning footprint. Springer Floor's established commercial account relationships and operational infrastructure could serve as an entry platform for Harvard to expand its national coverage into Kansas City and surrounding markets.
