Buyer Profile

Solenis is a global leader in specialty chemicals for water-intensive industries, currently owned by Platinum Equity following a $5.25 billion acquisition in 2021. The combined Solenis-Sigura Water platform generates approximately $3.5 billion in revenue across consumer, industrial, and pool water treatment markets, operating in 120+ countries with 5,200+ employees and 41 manufacturing facilities. Founded in 1907, Solenis is headquartered in Wilmington, Delaware.

Solenis's product portfolio spans innovative specialty chemicals and services for process, functional, and water treatment applications across two primary segments: Consumer Solutions (consumer and food packaging, graphic paper, tissue and towel) and Industrial Solutions (core water treatment and wastewater). The company's service-intensive model relies on over 1,400 highly trained sales technicians operating onsite at customer locations worldwide.

The strategic rationale for Solenis acquiring AquaScience is rooted in vertical integration. Solenis manufactures the specialty chemicals used in water treatment; AquaScience operates the treatment systems that consume these chemicals. Acquiring AquaScience would give Solenis a captive water treatment operation in Wyoming, providing both a testing ground for new chemical formulations and a demonstration site for customer sales efforts.

Additionally, Solenis's merger with Sigura Water expanded the company into residential and commercial pool water and spa treatment markets, demonstrating a pattern of acquiring adjacent water treatment businesses to diversify the customer base. AquaScience's industrial and municipal water treatment focus in Wyoming would further diversify Solenis's market exposure while maintaining strategic coherence within the water treatment value chain.

Under Platinum Equity's ownership, Solenis has the financial backing and strategic mandate to pursue accretive acquisitions. The company's operational model -- with over 1,400 technicians operating onsite at customer locations -- is culturally compatible with AquaScience's hands-on treatment operations. An acquisition would strengthen Solenis's position in the U.S. West and add local market expertise in Wyoming's energy, mining, and agricultural water treatment needs.
