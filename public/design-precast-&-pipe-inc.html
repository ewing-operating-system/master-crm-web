Your Company Story

Design Precast & Pipe, Inc. is a premier family-owned precast concrete and pipe manufacturer founded in 2004 by Pat Fore III in Gulfport, Mississippi. What began as a local precaster focused on drainage structures and sewer manholes has evolved into a regional powerhouse spanning the entire Gulf Coast corridor. The company's trajectory from a single Mississippi facility to a two-plant operation — adding an Arnaudville, Louisiana location — demonstrates the kind of disciplined, organic growth that acquirers prize. With an estimated $15 million in annual revenue and a workforce between 31 and 52 employees depending on project load, Design Precast operates at the sweet spot where owner-operator excellence meets scalable infrastructure. The company's product portfolio is exceptionally broad for its size: reinforced concrete pipe (RCP), reinforced concrete arch pipe (RCAP), reinforced box culvert (RCB), catch basins, drainage inlets, manholes, lift stations, sanitary sewer products, storm drainage products, shoreline protectors, custom precast structures, electrical structures, marine structures, and design-build services. Their motto — 'if you can think it, we can make it' — reflects genuine custom fabrication capability, not just catalog sales. This breadth insulates them from single-product-cycle risk and makes them a one-stop shop for contractors across the southern United States. Perhaps most significant is the company's proprietary intellectual property: the patented Lifting Eye technology developed through PF3 Global / DPPI. This is a genuine engineering innovation in how reinforced concrete pipe and arch pipe are handled and installed — a safety and efficiency advancement that no competitor can replicate without licensing. Patented technology in a traditionally commodity-driven industry is rare and creates defensible margin advantage. The company's service territory — Houston, Texas to Tallahassee, Florida and everything in between — covers one of the fastest-growing infrastructure corridors in the United States, anchored by two strategically positioned manufacturing plants. Growth signals are strong across multiple indicators: 15.8% year-over-year LinkedIn employee growth, 20.2% yearly follower growth reaching 2,049 followers, and expansion from a single-state operation to a multi-state regional manufacturer. The family structure — with Pat Fore III as President/Owner and E.F. A 4.0-star Google rating across 25 reviews reflects solid market reputation with room for further brand development.

Your Market Landscape

The precast concrete and pipe manufacturing sector is experiencing a historic wave of consolidation driven by three converging forces: massive federal infrastructure spending (IIJA/Bipartisan Infrastructure Law), aging municipal water and sewer systems requiring replacement, and strategic acquirers seeking to build regional platforms. The landmark transaction in this space was CMC's acquisition of Concrete Pipe & Precast LLC for $675 million in December 2025 — a deal valued at approximately 9.5x EBITDA that sent a clear signal about the premium valuations available for scaled precast/pipe platforms. Nucor Corporation has also been actively acquiring in the space, purchasing both Concrete Pipe & Precast (CP&P) and Foley Products Company to expand its Construction Solutions Group, while Alcrete acquired Cape Fear Precast in 2023, demonstrating sustained buyer appetite specifically in the Southeast. These transactions reveal a clear acquirer playbook: large strategic buyers and private equity platforms are assembling regional precast networks by acquiring family-owned operators with strong local market positions, established contractor relationships, and manufacturing infrastructure that would take years to replicate through greenfield development. The economics are compelling — precast concrete products are heavy and expensive to ship long distances, creating natural geographic moats around manufacturing facilities. A plant in Gulfport and a plant in Arnaudville effectively control a significant portion of the Gulf Coast precast supply chain. Median EBITDA multiples for the broader concrete/precast sector range from 2.5x to 6.0x, with revenue multiples between 0.3x and 1.0x, but premium assets with proprietary technology, multi-location operations, and strategic geography are commanding the upper end of these ranges. The buyer universe for Gulf Coast precast assets is deep and motivated. Strategic acquirers include national precast platform companies (Forterra, Quikrete/U.S. Pipe, RINKER), large building products conglomerates (CMC, Nucor, Summit Materials), and infrastructure-focused private equity firms building roll-up platforms. Financial buyers — particularly PE firms executing infrastructure roll-ups — are actively seeking bolt-on acquisitions in the $10M-$50M revenue range where they can acquire at lower multiples and benefit from platform-level valuation arbitrage. The federal infrastructure spending tailwind has at least 5-7 years of remaining runway, making this a secular growth story rather than a cyclical one.
