Strategic Assessment

Geographic Logic: Gainey's sits in Livingston Parish, about 100 miles from Design Precast's Arnaudville, LA facility. That is close enough for shared customer relationships, shared supplier terms, and shared LDOTD certifications — but far enough that the plants are not redundant. Holden serves the Baton Rouge–New Orleans corridor; Arnaudville serves the Acadiana market. Combined, Design Precast would own the Louisiana precast corridor from I-49 to the Gulf Coast. Product Overlap is a Feature: Gainey's wastewater and stormwater focus is exactly what Design Precast already does. This is not a capability bolt-on — it is a market share acquisition. Day-one revenue is predictable because the product lines are the same. No retraining, no product line integration risk. Succession Thesis: A 44-year-old family business in a physically demanding industry is a high-probability succession candidate. If the original Gainey founder is in their 60s or 70s, the question is not whether they exit — it is whether a buyer is in the room when they decide to.
